Overview

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cI have no idea when I\'ll turn into a monster again. If that scares you, you should end our contract while you still can.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Accompanied by her majestic Pegasus, Medusa appears in her original goddess form, though most of her abilities originate from her monstrous side. Manifesting in the Rider class, she is a Quick Servant with an AoE NP who excels in farming (Caster) enemies quickly.

\n

Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s main selling point is Blood Fort Andromeda\xe2\x80\x99s NP Charge, which allows her to perform instant NPs with the help of Starting NP CEs. Thanks to the multiplicative buffs of Monstrous Strength and Riding, Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s NP Bellerophon\xc2\xa0packs a decent punch as well, which altogether\xc2\xa0makes her an ideal farming Servant. An aspect that is further improved after gaining access to her NP Upgrade. Blood Fort\xe2\x80\x99s secondary effect also improves her long-term NP gain, which synergizes well with\xc2\xa0her high critical star weight and improved critical damage from Independent Action. Last but not least, Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s Mystic Eyes skill grants her access to an unrestricted Stun, an important asset when she is brought to a harder stage.

\n

Unfortunately for Medusa, she also harbors a couple of glaring weaknesses, the most prominent of which is her overall unsuitability for dealing sustained single-target damage. Outside of her AoE NP, Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s QQAAB deck and shorter-duration-than-usual attack buff leave her with no way to quickly finish off high health enemies. Additionally, while her Stun is undeniably powerful, its single target effect is at odds with her AoE nature, and it is generally unimportant for farming, which is where she thrives. Finally, Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s low Quick hit count makes her star generation rather poor despite her NP\xe2\x80\x99s buff, while her lack of\xc2\xa0other survival skills make her less appealing for longer fights as well.

\n

Above all, Medusa is best utilized as a budget farming Servant, thanks to her NP charge skill and decent AoE damage. However, despite possessing\xc2\xa0a powerful Stun, her low single-target damage and lack of survivability makes her unsuitable for harder fights, in particular those with beefy single bosses.

\n

Strengths

\n
\n

Instant NP and Decent NP Gain

\n

Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s NP deals decent damage, especially after her NP Upgrade, and Blood Fort Andromeda\xe2\x80\x99s NP Charge allows her to unleash it early in a fight when equipped with Starting NP Gauge CEs. This makes her fairly effective\xc2\xa0for 3-turning Caster-heavy stages, such as QP daily quests. Blood Fort\xe2\x80\x99s secondary effect, along with her star weight, improves on her decent Arts NP gain and allows her to gain NP pretty reliably

\n

Universal Stun

\n

While the Stun debuff is most commonly used to prevent an enemy from using their NP for one turn, it also prevents the target from dealing any damage, and it can even be used to foil a stage\xe2\x80\x99s gimmicks on rare occasions. Mystic Eyes is one of the few Stun skills with no restriction, letting Medusa take a turn away from virtually any enemy in the game.

\n
\n

Weaknesses

\n
\n

Low Single-Target Damage

\n

While her NP Damage is acceptable, Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s damage potential is crippled by her QQAAB deck, which is difficult to deal damage with. Although Monstrous Strength and her passives provide a respectable increase in overall damage, they can only do so much to help her in this regard. She often struggles to dish out enough damage as a result, especially against single, high HP bosses.

\n

No Survivability Skills

\n

Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s Stun allows her to reduce incoming damage and delay enemy NPs. Against powerful NPs, however, simply delaying them is often not enough, and Medusa relies on external help to provide her with defensive solutions. Additionally, as Stuns are often kept for emergencies, her long-term sustainability is also rather poor.

\n

Subpar Star Generation

\n

Despite having two Quick cards and a Quick NP, their low hit counts mean that Medusa won\xe2\x80\x99t be producing a considerable amount of critical stars or NP charge with them. While her NP provides a critical star generation buff, its small percentage makes little difference overall for her even after her NP upgrade, and an NP Quick Brave chain is the only time she will generate a decent amount of stars. However, after her NP upgrade her NP can be used to boost the star generation of high hit count allies.

\n
\n

\n\nCute Orangette
\n\nImaginary Around
\n
\n
\n
\n

As Medusa is a Quick-oriented Servant, Quick Performance Up Craft Essences are an obvious safe pick. Her other options are generally aimed at improving her NP or her critical damage. The former utilizes CEs with effects such as Starting NP Gauge or NP Damage Up, and the latter is achieved by using Critical Damage Up CEs.

\n
  • \n

    Kaleidoscope / The Imaginary Element: Best used in farming scenarios, these CEs, aided by Blood Fort Andromeda, are intended to allow access to Bellerophon early in the fight, potentially on the very first turn.

    \n
    • \n
  • \n

    Holy Night Supper / Knights of Marines / Golden Sumo: Boulder Tournament: Hybrid CEs grant Medusa some permanent improvements, while still allowing her to unleash Bellerophon relatively early. These should be used instead of the above CEs when NP-charging allies are brought along.

    \n
    • \n
  • \n

    The Black Grail / Origin Bullet: The massive damage boosts granted by these CEs stack multiplicatively with Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s own buffs, making them her best choices in terms of damage increase. The Black Grail is the go-to choice if all she needs is a deadly AoE nuke, while Origin Bullet is a more flexible choice for Caster-heavy stages.

    \n
    • \n
  • A Moment of Tranquility / Knight\xe2\x80\x99s Dignity / Cute Orangette / Imaginary Around: These CE picks aim to improve Medusa\xe2\x80\x99s normal cards in some way. Choices with Quick Performance Up and NP Damage Up effects also benefit her NP, resulting in a more balanced performance.
