Be like a falling leaf! But don\xe2\x80\x99t actually fall!

\n

Riding on a Hippogriff and wielding a golden lance, Astolfo has escaped from his myrtle tree prison to destroy the evil Casters who trapped him there. Now freed, Astolfo is a powerful farming Servant with his decent hitting AoE Quick Noble Phantasm that can be charged instantly with a little help.

\n

Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s main strengths lie in his rapid-cast Noble Phantasm. By using Evaporation of Reason after its Skill Upgrade, Astolfo gains a 50% Noble Phantasm charge with which to unleash Hippogriff fast. A combination of the Attack Buff from Monstrous Strength, Riding A+, and his NP Upgrade, ensures this Noble Phantasm can be used for decent AoE damage. Astolfo also has significant survivability potential, thanks to a rare 3-hit evasion effect from his Noble Phantasm and a stun from Trap of Argalia. Lastly, Astolfo has good critical potential through a QQQAB deck, a Quick Noble Phantasm, the various random Critical effects in Evaporation of Reason, and the Instant Critical Stars generated by his Noble Phantasm.

\n

However, when Astolfo is not using his Noble Phantasm he struggles to perform. Astolfo is plagued with extremely low generation rates for both NP generation and Critical Star generation from his Command cards. To make matters worse, his critical skills are chance-based, and required to sufficiently damage an enemy outside of his Noble Phantasm. When this chance-based Critical potential fails to trigger, it exasperates his QQQAB deck\xe2\x80\x99s weaknesses and leads to generally low damage on his cards.

\n

Thus, Astolfo mainly serves as a Quick AoE burst useful for clearing waves and hopefully generating Critical Stars. As one of the few SR servants with a 50% NP charge and an AoE Noble Phantasm, he makes an excellent option on farming teams against Caster waves.

\n

Gameplay Tip 1

\nAstolfo\'s Evaporation of Reason is a powerful skill, yet one that behaves very differently from most almost all other chance-based skills (Fionn\'s Evasion is very similar). All of its buffs are always applied, but he only has a 65% chance of granting his associated buff or generating the stated critical stars that turn. Each turn re-calculates whether the buff actually grants what it is supposed to and Masters have no way of knowing whether the buffs are in effect until any attacks are done or the turn ends.\n

Gameplay Tip 2

\n

In terms of gameplay purposes, Astolfo is listed as genderless and will not be affected by anti-(fe)male effects or beneficial gender-based buffs. The one exception is Blackbeard\'s Gentlemanly Love, which does affect all genderless Servants as well.

\n

Strengths

\n
\n

Great Farming Potential

\n

Thanks to the 50% battery on Evaporation of Reason, Astolfo has no problem unleashing his Noble Phantasm without much external support. With an NP interlude to draw on, along with Monstrous Strength and a high rank of Riding, this Noble Phantasm can be used to great effect to wipe out enemy waves in a hurry.

\n

Strong Survivability

\n

Every time Astolfo uses his Noble Phantasm, he gains a 3-hit evasion buff which is not limited by turns. By timing his\xc2\xa0Noble Phantasm properly, he can wipe out an enemy wave and then evade subsequent boss attacks in the final wave with ease. Furthermore, his Trap of Argalia skill provides an on-demand stun to further reduce incoming damage, and he has\xc2\xa0access to Magic Resistance A which can help ward against crippling debuffs.

\n

Critical Potential

\n

With Evaporation of Reason Astolfo\xc2\xa0gains a 5-turn buff that\xc2\xa0potentially generates Critical Stars, a buff that potentially increases his Critical Star generation rate, and/or potentially increases his Critical damage. When added to the Critical Stars generated from his\xc2\xa0Noble Phantasm, his\xc2\xa0Independent Action B and the potential to Quick chain thanks to a QQQAB deck, Astolfo has the potential to both supply Critical Stars to the team and use him for decent damage.

\n
\n

Weaknesses

\n
\n

Poor Base Performance

\n

Astolfo is plagued with low hit counts, low NP regeneration rates, and low Critical Star generation rates. While these can be compensated for with his skills in one form or another, Astolfo will struggle to provide for the team when his\xc2\xa0skills are unavailable or fail to trigger. Furthermore, even an additional 50% Star Generation won\xe2\x80\x99t help Astolfo actively generate Critical Stars given how his Quick cards only have 2 hits.\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0

\n

Chance Based Skills

\n

Outside of Monstrous Strength, all of Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s skills have some element of chance involved in him. Trap of Argalia has a 10% chance to miss at max level even before factoring in enemy resistances, and Evaporation of Reason\xe2\x80\x99s Critical buffs each have an independent 35% chance of missing. Due to how Evaporation of Reason resets each turn and recalculates whether each of its buffs are in effect, Astolfo is rather unreliable when it comes to his Critical niche. Furthermore,\xc2\xa0Astolfo relies on these buffs to be effective outside of farming, and missing even one would be crippling to his output.

\n

Low Sustained Damage

\n

Astolfo fails to output significant damage in general. While his Noble Phantasm can hit relatively good at high NP levels, its damage is otherwise on the lower side (but still adequate) compared to Welfare and NP5 3* Servants. Outside of his NP, he relies heavily on his chance based skills to generate significant damage, and when those buffs fail to trigger he is\xc2\xa0stuck with a subpar hit count QQQAB deck. Furthermore, his Monstrous Strength only provides a 1-turn weak attack buff, which Masters will likely use on his\xc2\xa0NP turn instead of helping with his Card damage.

\n
\n

Masters will most likely be using Astolfo as a \xe2\x80\x9ccharge and fire\xe2\x80\x9d servant. As such, it is practically mandated to level Evaporation of Reason to 10 for the full 50% charge. Monstrous Strength pairs well with Evaporation of Reason, but could be left at 6 due to low scaling. Trap of Argalia only increases stun chance, and can be left for last and at lower skill levels.

\n
  • \n

    Monstrous Strength C- is Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s primary steroid, providing him with a1-turn attack buff. While more damage is always good to hit certain damage benchmarks when farming, Masters may wish to consider whether the extra attack on an otherwise low-attack servant is worth upgrading further. Level this second.

    \n
    • \n
  • \n

    Trap of Argalia D is quite useful to stall an enemy servant for a turn via its stun. However, it has a relatively high stun rate at base, and never hits the full 100% even at max, ending at 90%. As it has a huge cooldown to go with it, there is little to gain from leveling this skill. Masters can leave this skill for last.

    \n
    • \n
  • Evaporation of Reason D+ is Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s bread and butter, supplying him with up to a 50% NP charge after his\xc2\xa0Interlude. This charge is the difference between firing a Noble Phantasm and not for Astolfo, and should always be leveled to max. In addition, it greatly boosts Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s CE flexibility. The secondary effects of boosting the effects of Critical Damage buff, Critical Stars per turn, and Critical Star Generation buff are nice, but are merely extras given how unclear it is whether the buffs are actually in effect. Masters should level this skill first.

Craft Essence Recommendation

\n
\n

\n\nKnights of Marines
\n\nGolden Sumo: Boulder Tournament
\n\nDragon's Meridian
\n\nSummertime Mistress
\n\nHot Spring Under the Moon
\n\nKnight's Dignity
\n\nRecord Holder
\n
\n
\n
\n

As Astolfo should have access to a 50% Noble Phantasm Charge, he will want to use CEs which provide the other 50% and a secondary effect. As such, Mixed CEs or Starting NP Gauge CEs will give him both immediate access to his Noble Phantasm and a steroid. Lacking that, a Critical Up CE could help Masters interested in using his chance-based Critical potential. Masters could also consider lowering Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s chance-based skills with Debuff Success Rate CEs.

\n
  • \n

    Holy Night Supper / Knight of the Marines / Golden Sumo: Boulder Tournament: With a 50% battery, Astolfo wants the other 50% from a CE with a secondary effect. Holy Night Supper is preferable for also increasing his\xc2\xa0Critical Damage.

    \n
    • \n
  • \n

    Dragon\xe2\x80\x99s Meridian: The budget version of a 50% NP Gauge CE, Dragon\xe2\x80\x99s Meridian will provide the missing 50% necessary fire Astolfo\xe2\x80\x99s NP.\xc2\xa0

    \n
    • \n
  • \n

    Summertime Mistress / Hotspring Under the Moon / Knight\xe2\x80\x99s Dignity: If a master wants to use Astolfo for his Critical potential, a Critical Damage CE does exactly that.

    \n
    • \n
  • Record Holder: Astolfo at max has a 90% chance to stun the enemy with Trap of Argalia, and Record Holder can provide the remaining 10% and then some.
